2.3 4. Ensure inclusive and equitable quality education and promote lifelong learning opportunities for all
  • The local teacher(s) can complete their study via distance learning. Later they can get in-service training and coaching, often online.
  • To teachers / students from the city, the settlements become more attractive to live in.
  • More teaching staff enables preprimary education as well as adult education via (blended learning).
  • In anticipation of a curriculum change, extra activities for technical and vocational skills will be organised (computerclubhouse, makerspace).
  • Children will be prepared for secondary education (elsewhere) and stay in touch with home via ict.
  • Only the availablility of radio and TV will make the people more used to our national language.
  • As more distance learning programs become available, the teachers will act as organizers, facilitators and coaches for the people in the village.
